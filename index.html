The market challenge

Three problems your current ingredients can't solve together

Formulators face converging pressures. Existing SPF boosters solve one problem but create others. Multifunctional = formulation problem-solver.

Greasy SPF formulas

Today's leading SPF boosters are oil-phase esters. Higher SPF = greasier texture. Consumers rank greasiness as the #1 reason they skip sunscreen. KAFFAGE® is water-soluble — it boosts SPF by 58% at 1% without adding any oiliness.

White cast from mineral filters

Consumers distrust chemical UV filters. Brands are shifting to ZnO/TiO₂ — but mineral means white cast, especially on deeper skin tones. KAFFAGE® naturally tints the formulation, eliminating white cast while boosting protection.

Too many single-function actives

Formulators juggle 8–12 ingredients per SKU. Each adds cost, stability risk, and complexity. KAFFAGE® delivers SPF boosting + antioxidant + anti-ageing + hydration + tone correction in one water-soluble active at 1%. One ingredient, five functions — true multifunctionality that simplifies your formula.

The Ingredient

Melanoidins — a new class of cosmetic active

Melanoidins are high-molecular-weight (200 Da – 300 kDa), water-soluble, nitrogen-containing biopolymers formed naturally during coffee roasting through the Maillard reaction.

Their macromolecular backbone incorporates galactomannans, arabinogalactan-proteins, and chlorogenic acid derivatives (caffeic acid, ferulic acid). Phenolic, carbonyl, and amino functional groups give them anionic and amphiphilic character, potent antioxidant capacity, and metal chelation ability.

Melanoidins constitute ~25–28% of roasted coffee bean dry weight. Kaffe Bueno is the first and only company globally to industrialise melanoidins for cosmetic use.

Despite the novelty of the ingredient, we chose a clean, widely accepted INCI name — Coffea Arabica Seed Extract — for maximum regulatory compatibility and consumer acceptance across all markets.

Our patented process (WO2021130349A1) isolates defined molecular weight fractions via alkaline aqueous extraction + MW fractionation — making melanoidins a programmable molecule with tuneable performance.

Mechanism of Action

Antioxidant-mediated biological photoprotection

The SPF-boosting mechanism operates through biological pathways — not UV filtration.

1. Radical scavenging — DPPH, ABTS, FRAP, ORAC activity [1,2,3]

2. Metal chelation — Binds Fe²⁺, Fe³⁺, Cu²⁺, preventing Fenton-type ROS amplification [4,5]

3. Chain-breaking — Hydrogen donation interrupts lipid peroxidation [6]

4. NF-κB / Nrf2 modulation — Suppresses pro-inflammatory cytokines [9,10,11]

Since SPF is measured by erythema (an inflammatory endpoint), ingredients that quench UV-induced ROS raise the Minimal Erythemal Dose — boosting SPF without acting as a UV filter. Same mechanism validated for vitamin E, ferulic acid, and Polypodium leucotomos [7,12].

Colour performance

KAFFAGE® naturally tints formulations — this is a feature, not a bug. At typical use levels (1–3%), the colour:

→ Eliminates white cast in mineral sunscreens
→ Provides a healthy, skin-matching glow
→ Does not stain skin at recommended levels
→ Rinses off easily with water

The colour is photostable — tested at an equivalent of 6 months indoor light exposure with no degradation observed.

KAFFAGE® is an active ingredient with inherent colour — not a pigment.
